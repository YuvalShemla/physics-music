# Open Questions & Decisions

## Decisions Needed

### 1. Station 6: Two Cage stations or swap one for Ives/Doppler?
- **Option A:** Keep both Cage stations (4'33" + Music of Changes) — different concepts, different physics
- **Option B:** Replace station 6 with Ives' *The Unanswered Question* / Doppler effect — discussed in class, more variety
- **Current lean:** Option B — Professor Susser explicitly discussed the Doppler/Ives connection in lecture

### 2. Station 10: Reich's Pendulum Music or Quantum Computer Music?
- **Option A:** End with Reich — simple, physical, visceral. "Gravity is the composer."
- **Option B:** End with Miranda's Qubism / quantum computing — forward-looking, 1863-to-2023 arc
- **Option C:** Combine both into a "Physics as Composer" finale
- **Current lean:** Option B — the 160-year arc from Helmholtz to quantum computing is more narratively powerful

### 3. Exhibition visual mood?
- Dark gallery (charcoal walls, spotlight on works) — dramatic, museum-like
- White cube gallery — clean, modern art space feel
- Abstract/physics-themed (grid lines, particle effects) — thematic but harder to build
- **Current lean:** Dark gallery with colored accent lighting per station

### 4. Audio strategy?
- Embedded YouTube players (legal, easy, but visually cluttered)
- HTML5 audio with custom play button (cleaner, needs hosted files)
- Auto-play on proximity (immersive, but can be annoying)
- **Current lean:** Click-to-play audio with visual waveform indicator
